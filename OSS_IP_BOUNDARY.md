# OSS/IP Boundary Policy

**Version**: 1.0
**License**: MIT (for OSS components)
**Date**: March 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This repository is the **open-source thinking toolkit** within the ARANGAM ecosystem.

This policy defines what is **Open Source (MIT)** vs **Proprietary IP**.

---

## 2. What is OSS (MIT)

- Methodology documentation (7 Pillars, 5 Sessions, Competence Stages, MBS, DDDE)
- YouTube video transcripts and diagrams (SVG)
- Blank thinking templates (/kit)
- Worked examples (anonymized)
- Setup guides and integration documentation
- Visual assets (pillar icons, session badges, thumbnail templates)
- NOOL/LOON lifecycle specifications

---

## 3. What is Proprietary IP

- **ARANGAM OS product code** (app.js, llm_router.js, github_bridge.js, insights.js)
- **Canonical data** (thirukkural.json verified corpus, twins.json persona registry)
- **AI agent logic** (LLM routing rules, RAG pipeline, prompt engineering)
- **Enterprise features** (team dashboards, SSO, multi-tenant infrastructure)
- **Backend services** (backend.py, rag_prototype.py)
- **Full persona prompts** (deep persona architectures from arangam-personas)
- **Books** (published content, narratives, illustrations)
- **Workshop content** (facilitator guides, student workbooks, assessment rubrics)
- **SPARKIT execution engine** (run_spar.py, TESSERACT auto-configuration)
- **Master research corpus** (2-research/ in synthai-master-repo)

---

## 4. Trademarks

Even when code/spec is MIT:

- **Trademarks are not granted**
- Names (SYNTHAI, ARANGAM, SPAR, CODEX) and logos are protected
- You may implement and adapt freely but may not claim "official" status without permission

---

## 5. The Boundary Rule of Thumb

> **If it teaches the principle or provides a blank structure → OSS (MIT)**
>
> **If it runs the automation, stores verified data, or provides hosted enterprise features → Proprietary IP**
