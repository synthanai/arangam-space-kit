# You Don't Have a System. You're Just Winging It.

> Session 1, Pillar 5: Build Systems | Build Your Legacy | ~4 min

---

## The Hook (0:00 - 0:05)

Tell me your system for learning new things.

Not what apps you use. Not what podcasts you listen to. Your actual system.

The step-by-step process you follow to go from "I just read something interesting" to "I can now use this knowledge reliably."

Can't describe it? That's because there isn't one.

## The Problem (0:15 - 1:30)

We all think we have systems. We don't. We have habits.

A habit is "I check email first thing in the morning." A system is "Every morning, I process my inbox using these three rules, and anything unresolved goes to this specific list with a deadline."

A habit is "I try to exercise regularly." A system is "I train MWF at 6am for 45 minutes, following this specific programme, and I track my progress weekly."

The difference? A habit depends on willpower. A system depends on architecture.

Here's the uncomfortable test. Pick any domain of your life: health, finances, learning, relationships, creativity.

Now try to write down your system for that domain. Not your goals. Not your aspirations. Your actual, repeatable process.

If you can't write it down in under five minutes, you don't have a system. You have vibes.

And vibes work great, until they don't. Until you're stressed, or tired, or distracted, and the whole thing collapses because there was nothing structural holding it together.

There's a concept from psychology that nails this.

---

## The Principle (1:30 - 2:45)

You're at Stage 1 of a five-stage competence ladder, and you don't even know it.

**Stage 1: Unconscious Incompetence.** You don't know what you don't know. You think your ad-hoc approach is working because you've never seen the alternative. This is where most people live. Not because they're lazy, but because nobody ever showed them the ladder.

Here's what the full ladder looks like:

- **Stage 1**: You don't have a system and you don't know it. (You're winging it.)
- **Stage 2**: You realize you don't have a system and it bothers you. (You buy an app.)
- **Stage 3**: You build a system but it takes enormous effort to maintain. (You're exhausted.)
- **Stage 4**: Your system runs effortlessly because you've internalized it. (It's invisible.)
- **Stage 5**: Your system is so good you stop improving it. (The trap.)

Right now, this video is designed to move you from Stage 1 to Stage 2. That's it. I just want you to SEE the gap.

Most professionals have Stage 4 competence in their craft (an architect can design a building without thinking about load calculations) but Stage 1 competence in running their own lives.

You're world-class at your work. And you're winging everything else.

The first step is not to build a system. The first step is to admit you don't have one.
