# Risk Ladder

> ⚠️ **STEAL THIS, DON'T COPY IT.**
> This is a simple framework for assessing how reversible your decisions are.

## Anti-Imitation Check
1. Am I assessing a real decision I'm currently facing?
2. Am I being honest about the true reversibility?

---

## The 5 Levels

| Level | Name | Description | Example | Action |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|--------|
| **L1** | Easily Reversible | You can undo this tomorrow with no lasting impact | Trying a new meeting format | Just do it. Don't overthink. |
| **L2** | Reversible with Effort | You can undo it, but it takes work and some cost | Switching project management tools | Try for 30 days. Set a review date. |
| **L3** | Partially Reversible | Some effects can be undone, others cannot | Reorganizing a team | Get input from affected people first. Document the rationale. |
| **L4** | Mostly Irreversible | Undoing this would be very costly or disruptive | Firing a key employee, shipping a product | Sleep on it. Get a second opinion. Write a decision record. |
| **L5** | Irreversible | Cannot be undone. The bridge is burned. | Shutting down a product line, public statements | Full dialectic debate. Multiple stakeholders. Written record. Clear walk-away conditions. |
