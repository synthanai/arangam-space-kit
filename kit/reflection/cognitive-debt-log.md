## Anti-Imitation Check
1. Am I logging real mistakes, not hypothetical risks?
2. Am I being honest about patterns I keep repeating?
3. Would a trusted colleague agree with my self-assessment?

---

## What is Cognitive Debt?

Just like technical debt (shortcuts in code that compound over time), cognitive debt is the growing cost of:
- Mistakes you didn't analyze
- Feedback you didn't internalize
- Patterns you didn't notice
- Lessons you refused to learn

The interest rate on cognitive debt is compounding. Each unlearned lesson makes the next failure more expensive.
